Exploring Artificial Intelligence

I am an assistant teaching professor at the University of Notre Dame. I mostly teach courses on artificial intelligence. This landing page is intended to serve both as a collection of my courses, and a list of materials that I myself am learning from. In addition to this ever lengthening list, I also maintain a modest library of books related to the topic, and encourage students to browse the library and come borrow books from me if they're interested.

Visualizations

Explore core AI algorithms through interactive visualizations. These tools are designed to help you understand how fundamental algorithms work by watching them execute step-by-step.

A* Pathfinding

Visualize the A* search algorithm finding optimal paths through grids. Compare heuristics like Manhattan, Euclidean, and Dijkstra.

Search Heuristics

Minimax & Alpha-Beta

Watch game trees expand with minimax and alpha-beta pruning. Play Tic-Tac-Toe and Nim against optimal strategies.

Game Theory Adversarial

K-Nearest Neighbors

Explore the KNN algorithm with interactive classification. Add training points, adjust K, and visualize decision boundaries.

Classification ML

Decision Tree

Build and visualize decision trees for classification. Watch how splits partition the feature space and explore tree structure.

Classification ML

Embeddings

Project MNIST and CIFAR-10 embeddings into 2D using PCA, t-SNE, and UMAP. Watch the embeddings evolve during training.

ML Visualization

Constraint Satisfaction

Solve Shidoku (4x4 Sudoku) with backtracking, AC-3, MRV, and MAC. Visualize search trees and arc consistency.

CSP Backtracking

Bayesian Networks

Explore probabilistic graphical models. Edit conditional probability tables and compute posterior probabilities given evidence.

Probabilistic Inference

Expert Systems

Learn forward and backward chaining inference. Watch rules fire step-by-step and see how conclusions are derived from facts.

Inference Knowledge

Perceptron Learning

Watch a perceptron learn a linear decision boundary step-by-step. Explore convergence and failure on non-separable data.

Classification Linear Models
